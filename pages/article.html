Spring Semester Registration Now Open

January 15, 2026
Communications Office
3 min read

Students can now register for Spring 2026 semester classes through Self-Service

Richmond Community College is excited to announce that registration for the Spring 2026 semester is now open. Students can access Self-Service to browse courses, build their schedule, and register for classes that fit their academic and professional goals.

Flexible Learning Options

The spring semester offers a wide variety of courses designed to accommodate different schedules and learning preferences. Whether you prefer traditional in-person classes, online learning, or a hybrid approach, Richmond CC provides options that work for you.

  • Day and Evening Classes: Choose times that fit your work and family schedule
  • Online Courses: Complete coursework from anywhere with full online sections
  • Hybrid Learning: Combine online and in-person instruction for maximum flexibility
  • Accelerated Formats: Complete courses in 8-week sessions to fast-track your degree

How to Register

Registration is easy through the Self-Service portal. Current students can log in with their existing credentials, while new students should complete the application process first. Here's what you need to do:

Registration Steps

  1. Meet with your advisor to plan your schedule and ensure course selections align with your degree plan
  2. Log into Self-Service using your student ID and password
  3. Search for classes by subject, instructor, time, or format
  4. Add courses to your cart and verify there are no time conflicts
  5. Submit your registration and confirm your enrollment
  6. Make payment arrangements or apply financial aid to cover tuition

Important Dates

Early Registration Begins January 15, 2026
Late Registration Period February 10-14, 2026
Classes Begin February 17, 2026
Drop/Add Deadline February 24, 2026
Last Day to Withdraw April 30, 2026

Need Help?

If you have questions about registration, course selection, or need technical assistance with Self-Service, our team is here to help. Contact the Advising Center at (910) 410-1700 or visit us in person at the Student Services building.
